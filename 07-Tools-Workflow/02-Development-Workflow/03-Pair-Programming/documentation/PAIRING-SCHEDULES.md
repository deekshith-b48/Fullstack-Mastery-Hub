# Pairing Schedules

Effective pairing schedules balance pairing time with individual work. This guide covers different scheduling approaches and best practices.

## Scheduling Approaches

### Full-Time Pairing

**Approach:**
- Pair all day, every day
- No individual work time
- Constant collaboration

**Best For:**
- Complex projects
- Knowledge transfer focus
- Team building

### Part-Time Pairing

**Approach:**
- Pair for specific tasks
- Individual work for others
- Flexible schedule

**Best For:**
- Most teams
- Balanced approach
- Flexibility

### Scheduled Pairing

**Approach:**
- Fixed pairing hours
- Regular schedule
- Predictable routine

**Best For:**
- Consistency
- Team coordination
- Remote teams

## Schedule Patterns

### Daily Patterns

**Morning Pairing:**
- Pair in mornings
- Individual work afternoons
- Fresh start

**Afternoon Pairing:**
- Individual work mornings
- Pair in afternoons
- Collaboration focus

**Alternating:**
- Alternate pairing days
- Mix of individual and pairing
- Flexibility

### Weekly Patterns

**Task-Based:**
- Pair for complex tasks
- Individual for simple tasks
- Task-driven

**Rotation:**
- Rotate pairing partners
- Different pairs daily
- Knowledge sharing

**Project-Based:**
- Pair for specific projects
- Individual for others
- Project-driven
