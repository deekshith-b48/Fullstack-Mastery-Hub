# Agile Best Practices

Agile best practices help teams succeed with Agile. This guide covers proven practices, common patterns, and recommendations for Agile success.

## Best Practices Overview

Agile best practices include:
- **Team Practices**: Team collaboration practices
- **Process Practices**: Process improvement practices
- **Technical Practices**: Technical excellence practices
- **Communication Practices**: Communication practices
- **Continuous Improvement**: Improvement practices

## Team Practices

### Cross-Functional Teams

- **Skills**: Include all necessary skills
- **Self-Organizing**: Teams self-organize
- **Collaboration**: Encourage collaboration
- **Empowerment**: Empower teams

### Team Size

- **Optimal**: 5-9 team members
- **Too Small**: Less than 5 may lack skills
- **Too Large**: More than 9 reduces communication
- **Adjust**: Adjust based on context

### Co-location

- **Prefer Co-location**: Co-located teams work better
- **Remote**: Use tools for remote teams
- **Hybrid**: Hybrid approaches work
- **Communication**: Ensure good communication

## Process Practices

### Time-boxing

- **Strict Limits**: Enforce time limits
- **Sprint Duration**: Fixed sprint length
- **Event Duration**: Time-box all events
- **Respect**: Respect time-boxes

### Definition of Done

- **Clear Criteria**: Clear completion criteria
- **Team Agreement**: Team agrees on criteria
- **Enforced**: Enforce consistently
- **Reviewed**: Review regularly

### Backlog Management

- **Prioritized**: Keep backlog prioritized
- **Refined**: Continuously refine backlog
- **Groomed**: Regular backlog grooming
- **Visible**: Make backlog visible
